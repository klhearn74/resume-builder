Something most job seekers don't know

What's actually reading your resume first?

It's not a person. It's software called an Applicant Tracking System (ATS) — used by most mid-size and large employers to scan every application for keywords and qualifications from the job posting, then rank candidates before a recruiter opens a single resume. If your resume uses different wording than the posting — even with the right experience — it can rank low and sit unseen at the bottom of the pile. That's often the real reason behind silence, not a lack of qualifications.
